This is just for you, Meg — not part of the public site. Do it once and every signed form lands in your inbox, in a spreadsheet you own forever, and a copy goes to whoever signed it. About 25 minutes total.

What's already working without doing anything: Every form emails you the instant it's submitted, and a backup is stored in your Netlify dashboard (downloadable as a spreadsheet anytime). The steps below add the two things you asked for — a copy you own, and an automatic email to the signer.

Part 1 — Confirm your email notifications 3 min

Make sure the forms are emailing you before anything else.

1

Go to app.netlify.com and open your Gracewood Acres site.

2

Click Forms in the top menu. After the next deploy you'll see all eight forms listed here (waiver, boarding-agreement, lesson-registration, and so on).

3

Go to Forms → Settings & usage → Form notifications → Add notification → Email notification.

Enter meg@gracewoodstables.com. Save. That's your inbox copy locked in.

Part 2 — Auto-save every form to a Google Sheet you own 15 min

This is the keeper — a spreadsheet in your Google account that no vendor can take away. We use a free connector called Zapier to copy each submission into it automatically.

1

In Google Drive, make a new Google Sheet. Name it something like Gracewood Forms — Master. Leave it open.

2

Go to zapier.com and make a free account (sign in with your Google login so it's tied to the same account as the Sheet).

3

Click Create → Zap. For the Trigger, search and pick Netlify, then event New Form Submission. Connect your Netlify account when asked.

4

Choose which form to watch. Start with the waiver — it's the one you most need a record of. You'll repeat this for the others in step 7.

5

For the Action, pick Google Sheets → Create Spreadsheet Row. Choose your Gracewood Forms — Master sheet.

6

Map the fields — Zapier shows every field from the form (name, email, signature, the dates, the agreement checkboxes). Match each to a column. Turn the Zap on.

Test it by submitting the waiver yourself once. A row should appear in your Sheet within a minute.

7

Repeat steps 3–6 for the other seven forms — or put them all in one Sheet on separate tabs. Tip: duplicate the first Zap and just change the form name; it's much faster than building each from scratch.

Don't want to use Zapier? Make.com works identically and has a more generous free tier. Or skip the connector entirely: once a month, open Netlify → Forms, click Download as CSV, and drop the file in a Google Drive folder. Five minutes, fully manual, equally safe. The connector just means you never have to remember.

Part 3 — Email a copy to the person who signed 7 min

Every form already collects the signer's email in an email field. We just add a step that sends them a confirmation.

1

Open the Zap you built in Part 2 (or make a new one with the same Netlify trigger).

2

Add an Action → Email by Zapier → Send Outbound Email (or Gmail → Send Email if you'd rather it come from your own address — looks more personal).

3

In the To field, select the form's email value from the dropdown. Write a short subject and body, e.g.:

Subject: We got your Gracewood Acres form
Body: Hi {{name}} — thanks! We've received your signed {{form-name}}. Keep this email for your records. Questions? Call or text (423) 607-5656. — Meg

The {{ }} bits are fields you pick from the dropdown; Zapier fills them in per submission.

4

Turn it on and test with your own email. Done — signers now get an instant confirmation, and you never send one by hand.

Troubleshooting — forms not showing in Zapier fix

Zapier can only see forms that Netlify already knows about. If the dropdown is empty, the problem is on the Netlify side — work down this list in order.

1

Redeploy the site first. Netlify scans for forms only when it builds. New form pages won't register until the next deploy. After deploying, open Netlify → Forms.

If the eight forms aren't listed there, Zapier will never see them — fix this before anything else.

2

Submit each form once yourself. A real test submission registers the form and gives Zapier a sample to map fields from. Forms with zero submissions often won't appear in the Zap editor.

3

Refresh the dropdown in Zapier. After the Netlify trigger, pick your site, then click the small refresh icon next to the form list. Zapier caches the list and won't show new forms until it re-queries.

4

Check the account. If you have more than one Netlify team or site, make sure the Zapier connection points at the Gracewood Acres site, not another one.

Quick test: if forms show in Netlify → Forms but still not in Zapier, the issue is the Zapier connection (steps 3–4). If they're missing from Netlify too, it's the deploy (step 1).

Your final setup

✓  Copy 1 — your inbox (Netlify email notification)
✓  Copy 2 — your Google Sheet (you own it, forever)
✓  Copy 3 — Netlify dashboard (downloadable CSV anytime)
✓  Copy 4 — the signer's own PDF + confirmation email

Four copies, at least two of which you control directly. If any single service disappeared, you'd still have the records. That's the "still have it in three years" guarantee.

One legal reminder: have your insurance agent or attorney glance at the waiver and the boarding/training releases once before relying on them. A short review now is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy.
